The Challenge

In robotics, success is often binary and delayed until the end of a trajectory[cite: 62, 81]. Standard calibration methods fail because they treat time-steps independently[cite: 77, 318]. We solve this by bridging Uncertainty Calibration and Reinforcement Learning[cite: 64, 65].

Method: TDQC

We introduce Temporal-Difference Q-based Calibration (TDQC). By minimizing a sequential version of the Brier score, we show the risk minimizer is actually the policy's value function[cite: 63, 89].

$$G(\theta) = \sum_{t=1}^{T-1}(f_{\theta}(h_t) - f_{\theta}(h_{t+1}))^2 + (f_{\theta}(h_T) - y)^2$$
